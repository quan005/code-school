<ProblemStatement title="A stack is a top-only pile">
  A stack is a data structure where we add to the top and remove from the top.
  The last thing we put in is the first thing we take out.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about a pile of plates">
  When you stack plates, the newest plate goes on top. When someone takes a
  plate, they also take the top one. That is how a stack works in code.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  Stacks are a clue when a problem cares about the most recent unfinished item,
  matching symbols, undo actions, or removing while the top matches a rule.
</InterviewTip>

## Three main actions

- <code>push</code> adds to the top.
- <code>pop</code> removes from the top.
- <code>peek</code> looks at the top without removing it.

## Lesson Summary

- A stack follows last in, first out.
- The top is the most important place.
- Push adds, pop removes, and peek looks.
- Stacks help when the newest unfinished thing matters most.
