<ProblemStatement title="A heap keeps one very important item easy to reach">
  A heap is a data structure that helps us quickly get the smallest or largest
  important item without sorting everything.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about a prize line">
  Imagine a contest where you only need to know the current highest score. You
  do not need every score in perfect order. You just need the top score ready.
  That is the kind of job a heap is good at.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  Heap problems often say top <code>k</code>, kth, most frequent, closest,
  cheapest, smallest, largest, or next best. Here, <code>k</code> just means how
  many.
</InterviewTip>

  time="Push and pop are often O(log n), peek is often O(1)"

## Two main kinds

- <strong>Min-heap</strong>: the smallest value is on top.
- <strong>Max-heap</strong>: the largest value is on top.

## Big reminder

A heap is <strong>not</strong> a fully sorted list. It mainly promises
something special about the top.

## Lesson Summary

- A heap keeps the most important item ready at the top.
- Min-heap means smallest on top.
- Max-heap means largest on top.
- <code>k</code> means how many.
- Heap clues often involve top-k, repeated min or max, or priority.
