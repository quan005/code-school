<ProblemStatement title="Can you rebuild the heap clues from memory?">
  This mastery check asks you to remember what heaps keep ready and how heap
  choice changes with the goal.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="The heap keeps the most important current item ready">
  That might mean the smallest item, the largest item, the farthest survivor to
  remove, or the weakest of the current top-k group.
</Intuition>

<ComplexityCard
  time="Heap push and pop are often O(log n)"
  space="Depends on how many candidates must be stored"
  notes="The big benefit is keeping one important item easy to reach without full sorting."
/>

## Reflection

Explain in your own words when you would choose a min-heap instead of a
max-heap.
