<ProblemStatement title="When should heap thinking appear?">
  This review lesson helps you remember the biggest clues that point to heaps.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="You often care about the top item, not full order">
  Heap problems often care about the next best, smallest, largest, or top-k
  item. That is different from wanting every value fully sorted.
</Intuition>

<ComplexityCard
  time="Often O(log n) per push or pop"
  space="Depends on how many candidates you keep"
  notes="The heap gives quick access to the most important item while avoiding full sorting."
/>

## Pattern Summary

- Top k largest: min-heap of size <code>k</code>
- Kth largest: min-heap of size <code>k</code>, heap top is the answer
- Top k frequent: count with a map, then heap the best entries
- K closest points: max-heap of size <code>k</code>
- Merge k sorted lists: min-heap of current front nodes
- Connect ropes: min-heap of rope lengths
