<ProblemStatement title="A graph shows things and the links between them">
  A graph is a collection of nodes connected by edges. In kid words, nodes are
  the dots and edges are the lines between them.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about rooms and hallways">
  Each room can be a node. Each hallway can be an edge. Then graph questions
  sound like, "Can I get from this room to that room?" or "Which rooms are in
  the same connected area?"
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  In this chapter, a <code>node</code> is one dot, place, or person. An
  <code>edge</code> is the connection between two dots. Graph clues often use
  words like connected, path, route, group, neighbor, dependency, and cycle.
</InterviewTip>

## Helpful graph words

- <strong>graph</strong>: a set of nodes and connections
- <strong>node</strong>: one dot in the graph
- <strong>edge</strong>: one connection line
- <strong>neighbor</strong>: a node directly connected to another node
- <strong>path</strong>: a route from one node to another

## Two common graph types

- <strong>Undirected</strong>: the connection goes both ways.
- <strong>Directed</strong>: the connection has a direction.

## Lesson Summary

- A graph shows nodes and edges.
- Nodes are the dots.
- Edges are the connections.
- Graph problems are often about routes, groups, and reachability.
- DFS and BFS are two big tools for walking through graphs.
