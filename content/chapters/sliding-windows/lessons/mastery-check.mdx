<ProblemStatement title="Final sliding-window check">
  This mastery check asks you to choose the right window style, explain your
  reasoning, and rebuild one last window helper without the walkthrough.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="What strong answers sound like">
  Strong answers explain what the window tracks and why it should stay fixed or
  change size. They do not only say “use left and right.”
</Intuition>

<HintDrawer title="Still unsure?">
  Remember the biggest clues: contiguous section, size k, longest, shortest,
  grow, shrink, and what must stay inside the window.
</HintDrawer>

## Part A: Multiple Choice

- Which clue suggests a fixed-size window?
- Which clue suggests a dynamic window?
- Which lesson uses a set inside the window?

## Part B: Short Answer

- What is the difference between a fixed-size and a dynamic window?
- Why can sliding windows be faster than brute force?
- Why does shrinking help in target or uniqueness problems?
