<ProblemStatement title="Can you rebuild the interval clues from memory?">
  This mastery check asks you to remember how starts, ends, overlap, and sorting
  work together in interval problems.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Ranges become easier after sorting">
  Once ranges are in order, you can compare neighbors, merge shared space, or
  count how many ranges are active at the same time.
</Intuition>

<ComplexityCard
  time="Often O(n log n) for interval work that starts with sorting"
  space="Depends on the helper data and result"
  notes="The important idea is not memorizing one answer, but recognizing how sorted ranges behave."

## Reflection

Explain in your own words why sorting by start time is often such a strong first
step in interval problems.
