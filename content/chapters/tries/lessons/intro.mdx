<ProblemStatement title="A trie is a word tree">
  A trie stores words one letter at a time so words with the same beginning can
  share part of the same path.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about many words starting the same way">
  If we store <code>cat</code>, <code>car</code>, and <code>cap</code>, all
  three start with <code>ca</code>. A trie keeps that shared beginning together,
  then splits only when the words become different.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  In this chapter, a <code>prefix</code> is just the beginning of a word. A
  <code>node</code> is one letter stop in the word tree. <code>isWord</code>
  means, "A whole word ends here."
</InterviewTip>

## Helpful trie words

- <strong>trie</strong>: a word tree
- <strong>prefix</strong>: the beginning of a word
- <strong>node</strong>: one letter stop
- <strong>children</strong>: the next possible letters
- <strong>isWord</strong>: a marker that says a whole word ends here

## Why tries are useful

- They help exact word search.
- They help prefix search.
- They help autocomplete-style jobs.
- They let many words share the same beginning path.

## Lesson Summary

- A trie is a word tree.
- A prefix is the beginning of a word.
- Words with the same beginning can share one path.
- <code>isWord</code> marks where a whole word ends.
- Trie clues often involve words, prefixes, and autocomplete.
