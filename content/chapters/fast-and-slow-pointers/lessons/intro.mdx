<ProblemStatement title="Two travelers, two speeds">
  Fast and slow pointers use one pointer that moves 1 step and another that
  usually moves 2 steps.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Picture two racers on a path">
  If the path ends, the fast racer reaches the end first. If the path loops, the
  fast racer eventually catches the slow racer. That one idea solves several
  important problems.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  Before doing <code>fast = fast.next.next</code>, always check that fast is not
  null and fast.next is not null.
</InterviewTip>

<ComplexityCard
  time="Usually O(n)"

## Big chapter clues

- Use this pattern when you see <strong>middle</strong>, <strong>cycle</strong>,
  <strong>loop</strong>, or <strong>repeats forever</strong>.
- The slow pointer usually moves 1 step.
- The fast pointer usually moves 2 steps.

## Lesson Summary

- Slow and fast move at different speeds.
- Different speeds can reveal a middle or a cycle.
- The fast pointer needs extra null checks for safety.
