<ProblemStatement title="Final fast-and-slow check">
  This mastery check asks you to explain what fast and slow movement tells you
  and to rebuild one last helper without the walkthrough.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="What strong answers sound like">
  Strong answers explain what the pointer speeds reveal. They do not just say
  “move fast by two.” They explain why that speed difference teaches us
  something.
</Intuition>

<HintDrawer title="Still unsure?">
  Remember the three biggest clues: fast reaching null, fast meeting slow, and
  slow landing near the middle.
</HintDrawer>

## Part A: Multiple Choice

- What does it mean if fast reaches null?
- What does it mean if fast meets slow?
- Which lesson treats a number process like a path of states?

## Part B: Short Answer

- Why does slow land near the middle when fast moves twice as quickly?
- Why must we check fast.next before moving fast by two?
- Why can Happy Number use the same pattern as a linked-list cycle?
