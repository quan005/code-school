<ProblemStatement title="A greedy algorithm makes the best choice it can right now">
  A greedy algorithm looks at the current moment, makes the best local choice it
  can, and then keeps moving forward.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about picking the best snack for your bag right now">
  You cannot test every future packing plan. Instead, you follow one rule and
  choose the best option you can right now. That is greedy thinking.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  A <code>greedy choice</code> is the best current move by the rule of the
  problem. It might mean smallest, largest, earliest finish, farthest reach, or
  the choice that protects future flexibility.
</InterviewTip>

## Helpful greedy words

- <strong>best now</strong>: the best current move by the problem’s rule
- <strong>local choice</strong>: one good move at the current step
- <strong>global answer</strong>: the final whole answer
- <strong>future flexibility</strong>: leaving good options open for later

## Strong greedy clues

- take the best current option
- finish as many as possible
- keep the farthest reach
- use the smallest helpful resource
- reset when the current plan becomes impossible

## Lesson Summary

- Greedy means choose the best move right now.
- Best now depends on the problem’s rule.
- Some greedy choices protect future flexibility.
- Greedy works for some problems, but not all of them.
- The key is knowing when the local rule can be trusted.
