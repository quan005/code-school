<ProblemStatement title="Mastery flow">
  This mastery check asks you to choose the right move, explain your reasoning,
  and finish one last coding task without the walkthrough.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="What strong answers sound like">
  Strong answers explain the clue behind the pattern. They do not just say "move
  left" or "move right." They explain why the move changes the search in the
  correct direction.
</Intuition>

<HintDrawer title="Still unsure?">
  Remember the chapter questions: What pattern can I trust? What does moving one
  pointer teach me? Which move keeps the answer possible?
</HintDrawer>

## Part A: Multiple Choice

- Which clue most strongly suggests Pair Sum - Sorted can use two pointers?
- In Largest Container, which pointer should move?
- Which problem uses unidirectional traversal?

## Part B: Short Answer

- What is a pointer?
- Why can sorted arrays help with two pointers?
- What job does the `write` pointer do in Shift Zeros to the End?

## Part C: Final Build

Use the mastery challenge below to rebuild the move rule from memory. Passing it
should mean you can explain and apply the most important chapter idea on your
own.
