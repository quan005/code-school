<ProblemStatement title="Look in the middle first">
  Binary search is a smart way to find something in sorted data. Instead of
  checking every spot, we check the middle and decide which half can be thrown
  away.
</ProblemStatement>

<Intuition title="Think about a dictionary">
  If you look for a word in a dictionary, you do not start on page 1. You open
  near the middle, see whether your word comes before or after, and then keep
  only the half that still makes sense.
</Intuition>

<InterviewTip>
  Binary search is usually a clue when the problem says sorted, ordered, first
  position, last position, insert position, or too high / too low.
</InterviewTip>

<ComplexityCard
  time="Often O(log n)"
  space="Often O(1)"
  notes="The big win is that one middle check can remove about half of the remaining choices."
/>

## Three helpful positions

- <code>left</code> is the start of the search area.
- <code>right</code> is the end of the search area.
- <code>mid</code> is the middle of the current search area.

## Core rule

- If the middle is exactly the target, we are done.
- If the middle is too small, move to the right half.
- If the middle is too large, move to the left half.

## Lesson Summary

- Binary search needs sorted order.
- The middle gives useful information.
- Left, right, and mid describe the current search area.
- Each smart comparison removes many choices at once.
